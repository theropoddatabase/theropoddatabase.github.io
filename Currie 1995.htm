Currie, 1995. New information on the anatomy and relationships of Dromaeosaurus albertensis (Dinosauria: Theropoda). Journal of Vertebrate Paleontology. 15(3), 576-591.

This paper redescribed the cranial anatomy of Dromaeosaurus, and has a small matrix designed more to show the proposed characters of that genus, velociraptorines and dromaeosaurids than to test a phylogeny.

Phylogeny

Currie's phylogeny was-

Theropoda
|--Herrerasaurus
|--Coelophysis
`--+--Sinraptor
   `--Dromaeosauridae
      |--Velociraptorinae
      |--Itemirus
      `--Dromaeosaurus

Taxon issues-

Coelophysis- This only includes C. bauri, as rhodesiensis is mentioned as Syntarsus.
Sinraptor- This includes both S. dongi and S? hepingensis, as Currie referred the latter to the genus the previous year.
Velociraptorinae- Currie states this includes Velociraptor, Saurornitholestes and Deinonychus.
