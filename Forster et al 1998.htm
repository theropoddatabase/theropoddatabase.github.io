Forster, Sampson, Chiappe and Krause, 1998a. The theropod ancestry of birds: New evidence from the Late Cretaceous of Madagascar. Science. 279, 1915-1919.

This paper described the paravian Rahonavis (as Rahona, which was quickly noted to be preoccupied by a moth), which was found to be an archaeopterygid in their decently-sized phylogenetic analysis of coelurosaurs.

Taxon issues-

Forster et al. never state which taxa are included in their suprageneric outgroups, so those taxa known at the time and commonly included are assumed here as specified below.
Tyrannosauridae- includes Gorgosaurus, Albertosaurus, Daspletosaurus, Alioramus, Tarbosaurus and Tyrannosaurus.
Ornithomimidae- includes Pelecanimimus, Harpymimus, Garudimimus, Archaeornithomimus, Anserimimus, Dromiceiomimus, Struthiomimus, Gallimimus and Ornithomimus.
Alvarezsauridae- includes Alvarezsaurus, Patagonykus, Parvicursor, IGM 100/99 and 100/120, Shuvuuia, and Mononykus.
Oviraptoridae- includes Oviraptor, IGM 100/42, Conchoraptor, Rinchenia and "Ingenia".
Troodontidae- includes IGM 100/44, Sinornithoides, Borogovia, Tochisaurus, Saurornithoides, Philovenator, Zanabazar and Troodon.
Rahona- Now known as Rahonavis.
Unenlagia- Includes U. comahuensis, U. paynemili and Neuquenraptor, now generally agreed to be synonymous.
Dromaeosauridae- includes Saurornitholestes, Velociraptor mongoliensis, Adasaurus, Deinonychus, Dromaeosaurus and Utahraptor.
Enantiornithes- includes Alexornis, Kizylkumavis, Sazavis, Avisaurus, Boluochia, Cathayornis, Concornis, Elbretornis, Enantiornis, Gobipteryx, Houornis, Martinavis spp., Nanantius, Neuquenornis, Otogornis, Sinornis, Soroavisaurus, Yungavolucris, LP-4450-IEI, Eoalulavis, and PVL coll. Lecho material.
Ornithurae- includes Ichthyornis, Enaliornis, Pasquiaornis, Baptornis, Parahesperornis, Hesperornis and Aves.

Coding issues-

1. Ornithomimidae should be polymorphic, as Harpymimus (Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005) and more derived taxa lack premaxillary teeth. Enantiornithes should also be polymorphic, as Gobipteryx (Chiappe et al., 2001) lacks premaxillary teeth.

2. This character should be ordered, and is here restricted to lateral teeth based on Forster et al.'s coding. Dromaeosaurids should be coded 0+1, since Deinonychus (Ostrom, 1969) and Dromaeosaurus (Currie et al., 1990) have mesial serrations on all teeth, and Saurornitholestes and Velociraptor (Barsbold and Osmolska, 1999) have them on some teeth. Troodontids should be scored 1, since basal forms Sinornithoides (Currie and Dong, 2001), Saurornithoides and Zanabazar (both Norell et al., 2009) lack mesial serrations. Compsognathus should be polymorphic 1+2, as some specimens have posterior teeth which are unserrated (Peyer, 2006). Alvarezsaurids (Shuvuuia- Dufeau, 2003; Mononykus- Perle et al., 1993), Enantiornithes (Cathayornis- Clarke, 2002; LP-4450-IEI- Sanz et al., 1997) and Ornithurae (Hesperornis- Marsh, 1880; Ichthyornis- Clarke, 2004) should be coded 2. Oviraptorids should be inapplicable since they lack teeth.

3. This is a composite character, combining lack of posterior curvature with lack of labiolingual compression. Only the former is coded for here. Ornithurae (Ichthyornis- Clarke, 2004; Hesperornis- Marsh, 1880) have recurved teeth. Archaeopteryx (Howgate, 1984) and enantiornithines (0 in Boluochia- O'Connor et al., 2011) are polymorphic. Ornithomimidae (Pelecanimimus- unpublished photo) have unrecurved teeth. Oviraptorids should be inapplicable since they lack teeth.

4. Compsognathus (Rauhut, 2003), Ornithomimidae (Harpymimus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005) and Archaeopteryx (Elzanowski and Wellnhofer, 1996) are polymorphic. Oviraptorids should be inapplicable since they lack teeth.

5. Compsognathus has jugal-antorbital fenestra contact (Peyer, 2006). Oviraptoridae (no contact in Oviraptor- Clark et al., 2002) is polymorphic.

6. The states are reversed as coded. Allosaurus (Chure, 2000) lacks naris-maxilla contact. The condition in Compsognathus is unknown (Peyer, 2006; Ostrom, 1978).

7. Tyrannosauridae (Gorgosaurus and Albertosaurus- Carr, 2003; Daspletosaurus- Russell, 1970) and Alvarezsauridae (Shuvuuia- Sereno, 2001) have transverse nasofrontal sutures. Ornithomimidae (transverse in Pelecanimimus- unpublished photos) and Oviraptoridae (acute in IGM 100/42- unpublished photos) are polymorphic. Patagopteryx and Ornithurae should be inapplicable as they lack median frontal-nasal contact.

8. Alvarezsaurids have a large prefrontal (Shuvuuia- Sereno, 2001). Dromaeosaurids (Adasaurus- Turner et al., 2012; Deinonychus- Paul, unpublished; Velociraptor- Barsbold and Osmolska, 1999) have reduced prefrontals.

9. Allosaurus (Gilmore, 1920), Compsognathus (Ostrom, 1978; Peyer, 2006) and Dromaeosauridae (Velociraptor- Barsbold and Osmolska, 1999; Deinonychus- Paul, unpublished) have a frontal over 150% of parietal length.

10. Archaeopteryx (Alonso et al., 2004) lacks a sagittal crest. Patagopteryx (Chiappe, 2002) has one. Ornithurines are polymorphic (crest in Enaliornis- Galton and Martin, 2002).

11. This is quantified as a suborbital jugal height 12% or less of orbital height.

12. Enantiornithes (LPP-4450-IEI- Sanz et al., 1997) have a dorsal jugal process.

13. Oviraptorids (Conchoraptor- Elzanowski, 1999; IGM 100/42 and Rinchenia- both coding in Maryanska et al., 2002) lack a subsidiary palatal fenestra. Tyrannosaurids (Gorgosaurus, Albertosaurus and Daspletosaurus- all Currie, 2003) and alvarezsaurids (Shuvuuia- Dufeau, 2003) have the fenestra.

14. This character should be ordered. Compsognathus (Peyer, 2006), alvarezsaurids (Shuvuuia- Dufeau, 2003) and Enantiornithes (Sinornis- Sereno et al., 2002; Gobipteryx- Elzanowski, 1995) at least have ectopterygoids. Troodontids (Saurornithoides- Norell et al., 2009) lack ectopterygoid-palatine contact, but have ectopterygoids.

15. Oviraptorids (Conchoraptor- Maryanska and Osmolska, 1997; Rinchenia and IGM 100/42- both coding in Maryanska et al., 2002), troodontids (Sinornithoides- Russell and Dong, 1994; Zanabazar- Norell et al., 2009; Troodon- Currie and Zhao, 1994) and Archaeopteryx (Elzanowski, 2002) have quadrate-braincase contact.

16. Compsognathus (Peyer, 2006), alvarezsaurids (Shuvuuia- Sereno, 2001), dromaeosaurids (Adasaurus- Turner et al., 2012; Velociraptor- Barsbold and Osmolska, 1999; Dromaeosaurus- Currie, 1995), Patagopteryx (Chiappe, 2002) and ornithurines (Hesperornis; Gallus- Yasuda, 2002) lack posteriorly tilted quadrates. Troodontids (Sinornithoides- Russell and Dong, 1994; Saurornithoides- Norell et al., 2009) and Archaeopteryx (Paul, 2002) have posteriorly tilted quadrates (over 94 degrees).

17. Alvarezsaurids (Mononykus- coding in Hwang et al., 2004) lack twisted paroccipital processes. Tyrannosaurids (Gorgosaurus- Carr, 1996; Daspletosaurus- Currie, 2003) have twisted paroccipital processes. Dromaeosaurids (untwisted in Adasaurus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2006) and ornithurines (twisted in hesperornithines- Enaliornis Galton and Martin, 2002; Hesperornis- Elzanowski, 1991) are polymorphic. Compsognathus is unknown (Ostrom, 1978; Peyer, 2006).

18. Compsognathus (Ostrom, 1978), Archaeopteryx (Elzanowski and Wellnhofer, 1996) and Enantiornithes (Gobipteryx- Chiappe et al., 2001) lack a bulbous cultriform process.

19. Enantiornithes (LP-4450-IEI- Sanz et al., 1997) lack a lacrimal foramen. Dromaeosaurids have a foramen (Deinonychus- Witmer, 1997; Velociraptor- Barsbold and Osmolska, 1999). Troodontids are polymorphic, as the basal Sinornithoides has a lacrimal foramen (Currie and Dong, 2001).

20. Dromaeosaurids (Deinonychus- Paul, unpublished; some Velociraptor- Barsbold and Osmoska, 1999) have high nuchal crests.

21 The states are reversed as coded. Tyrannosaurids (Gorgosaurus- Carr, 1996; Albertosaurus- Currie, 2003; Alioramus- Kurzanov, 1976; Tarbosaurus- Hurum and Sabath, 2003; Tyrannosaurus- Brochu, 2003), alvarezsaurids (Shuvuuia- Dufeau, 2003) and troodontids (Zanabazar- Norell et al., 2009; Troodon- Currie, 1985) lack parietals fused to laterosphenoids.

22. Oviraptorids (Conchoraptor- Kundrat and Janecek, 2007), troodontids (Zanabazar- Norell et al., 2009) and dromaeosaurids (Dromaeosaurus- Currie, 1995; Utahraptor- Kirkland et al., 1993; Deinonychus- Paul, unpublished; Velociraptor- Barsbold and Osmolska, 1999) have a subnarial foramen. Enantiornithes (Gobipteryx- Chiappe et al., 2001) lack one. The condition in Compsognathus is unknown (Ostrom, 1978; Peyer, 2006).

23. Compsognathus (Peyer, 2006) lacks a quadratojugal cotyle in the quadrate. Oviraptorids (Oviraptor- Clark et al., 2002; IGM 100/42- Lu, 2004; Rinchenia and Conchoraptor- both coding in Maryanska et al., 2002) have a cotyle.

24. Ornithomimids (Garudimimus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005; Struthiomimus- Kobayashi, 2004; Dromiceiomimus- Tahara and Larsson, 2011), oviraptorids (Rinchenia and "Ingenia"- both Lu, 2004; IGM 100/42- coding in Norell et al., 2001; Conchoraptor- Kundrat and Janecek, 2007), troodontids (IGM 100/44- Barsbold et al., 1987; Troodon- Currie and Zhao, 1994) and Archaeopteryx (Alonso et al., 2004) have pneumatic quadrates.

25. Allosaurus (Gilmore, 1920), dromaeosaurids (Saurornitholestes- Sues, 1977; Velociraptor- Barsbold and Osmolska, 1999; Deinonychus- Ostrom, 1969; Dromaeosaurus- Currie, 1995; Utahraptor- Kirkland et al., 1993) and Archaeopteryx (Martin and Stewart, 1999; Foth et al., 2014) have fused or absent interdental plates. Oviraptorids should be inapplicable as they lack teeth.

26. Compsognathus (Peyer, 2006) has a coronoid. Ornithomimids (Garudimimus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005; Gallimimus- Hurum, 2001; Struthiomimus- coding in Norell et al., 2001), alvarezsaurids (Chiappe et al., 2002) and oviraptorids (IGM 100/42- Barsbold, 1983; Conchoraptor- coding in Maryanska et al., 2002; Rinchenia and "Ingenia"- both Lu, 2004) lack coronoids. Ornithurines are polymorphic (coronoid in Ichthyornis Clarke, 2004).

27. Enantiornithes are polymorphic (not heterocoelous in Gobipteryx- Kurochkin, 1996). Iberomesornis should be coded unknown, as its anterior cervicals are unknown, and only anterior cervicals are heterocoelous in some enantiornithines.

28. Alvarezsaurids are polymorphic (low hypapophyses in Alvarezsaurus- Bonaparte, 1991).

29. This matches the codings more closely if it's defined as >35%, not >40%. Dromaeosaurids are polymorphic (tall canals in Velociraptor- Norell et al., 2000; some Deinonychus vertebrae- Ostrom, 1969). The condition in coded oviraptorids and troodontids are unknown.

30. Though this character is stated to code for pneumatic dorsal vertebrae, the coding distribution clearly indicates it codes only for posterior dorsals, and this is assumed in the codings here. Archaeopteryx lacks pleurocoelous posterior dorsals (Mayr et al., 2007). Dromaeosaurids are polymorphic (no posterior pleurocoels in Velociraptor- Norell and Makovicky, 1999; Utahraptor- Britt et al., 2001). As posterior dorsals of Rahonavis (Forster et al., 1998), Iberomesornis (Sereno, 2000), enantiornithines and ornithurines have fossae the condition is uncertain.

31. Compsognathus has five sacrals (Peyer, 2006). Ornithomimids (Harpymimus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005; Garudimimus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005; Archaeornithomimus- Makovicky, 1995; etc.) and Iberomesornis (Sereno, 2000) have more than five sacrals. Dromaeosaurids (more than five sacrals in Adasaurus- unpublished photo; Velociraptor- Norell and Makovicky, 1999; Deinonychus- Ostrom, 1976) and Archaeopteryx (more than five sacrals in the Thermopolis specimen- Mayr et al., 2007) are polymorphic.

32. This character codes for complete sacral fusion in adults, but this is present in all avetheropods where it can be confirmed. Thus it is excluded here and the fourteen coded taxa are recoded unknown.

33. This character should be ordered. In addition, there are gaps between states so that taxa with 26-29 or 16-19 caudals cannot be coded. To solve this the intermediate state is expanded to include taxa with 16-29 caudals. Alvarezsaurids have more than 29 caudals (IGM 100/120- Suzuki et al., 2002). Enantiornithines (Sinornis and Cathayornis- both Sereno et al., 2002) have ~19 caudals. Oviraptorids are polymorphic (less than 30 caudals in Rinchenia and "Ingenia"- both Barsbold et al., 2000). Troodontids should be coded 0/1, since Sinornithoides has slightly more than 27 caudals, but an unknown exact number (Russell and Dong, 1994). Similarly, Rahonavis preserves 13 caudals and certainly had more than 2 more, but is coded 0/1.

34. Oviraptorids are polymorphic (pygostyle in Conchoraptor- Persons et al., in press). Compsognathus, alvarezsaurids, troodontids and Rahonavis do not preserve tail tips, so cannot be coded for pygostyle presence.

35. Compsognathus (Peyer, 2006), alvarezsaurids (Alvarezsaurus- Bonaparte, 1991; Shuvuuia- pers. obs.), troodontids (Sinornithoides- Currie and Dong, 2001; Zanabazar- Norell et al., 2009) and dromaeosaurids (Velociraptor- Norell and Makovicky, 1999; Deinonychus- Ostrom, 1969) have eleven or more caudals with transverse processes. Iberomesornis has ten or less (Sereno, 2000).

36. Iberomesornis, Enantiornithes and Ornithurae should be coded inapplicable due to their short tails.

37. Alvarezsaurids are polymorphic due to parvicursorines. The condition in Compsognathus is unknown (Ostrom, 1978; Peyer, 2006).

38. Unenlagia (coding in Turner, 2008) and Rahonavis (coding in Norell et al., 2001) have sacral pleurocoels. Allosaurus (Chure, 2000), Compsognathus (Peyer, 2006), ornithomimids (Harpymimus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005; Garudimimus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005; Archaeornithomimus- Makovicky, 1995; etc.), troodontids (Saurornithoides- pers. obs.; Troodon- Makovicky, 1995) and Iberomesornis (Sereno, 2000) lack sacral pleurocoels.

39. This is a composite character coding for neural spine length and height. Only height is coded for here. Compsognathus (Peyer, 2006), oviraptorids (Oviraptor- pers. obs.; IGM 100/42 and Rinchenia- both unpublished photos; "Ingenia"- Makovicky, 1995; Conchoraptor- coding in Maryanska et al., 2002) and Archaeopteryx (Wellnhofer, 1976) have low cervical neural spines.

40. Compsognathus (Ostrom, 1978) and alvarezsaurids (long caudals in Alvarezsaurus- Bonaparte, 1991) are polymorphic. Iberomesornis, Enantiornithes and Ornithurae should be coded inapplicable due to their short tails.

41. This character leaves a gap between prezygapophyses 25% of centrum length and 50%, which is solved here by changing the cutoff point between states to 33%. Compsognathus (Peyer, 2006) has distal caudal prezygapophyses <33% of centrum length. Iberomesornis, Enantiornithes and Ornithurae should be coded inapplicable due to their short tails. The condition in Patagopteryx is unknown (Chiappe, 2002).

44. Alvarezsaurids (Alvarezsaurus- Bonaparte, 1991; Shuvuuia- pers. obs.; Mononykus- Perle et al., 1994), troodontids (Sinornithoides- Currie and Dong, 2001; Troodon- Varricchio et al., 2002), dromaeosaurids (Saurornitholestes- Jasinoski et al., 2006; Velociraptor- Norell and Makovicky, 1999) and Iberomesornis (Sereno, 2000) have an anteriorly angled acromion. Oviraptorids (angled in IGM 100/42, Rinchenia and "Ingenia"- all unpublished photos) are polymorphic.

45. Unenlagia (Novas et al., 1997) has an unmobile scapulocoracoid joint.

46. This character should be ordered. Alvarezsaurids (Alvarezsaurus- Bonaparte, 1991; Patagonykus- Novas, 1997; Shuvuuia- pers. obs.; Mononykus- Perle et al., 1994) lack a large biceps tuber on the coracoid. Compsognathus lacks an acrocoracoid process, but the size of its biceps tuber is unknown (Peyer, 2006).

47. The apparent sharp angle between the coracoid and scapula in ornithothoracines is due to their lacking the proximal coracoid portion which is at a wide angle to the scapula in other theropods, such as Archaeopteryx (Mayr et al., 2007).

48. This character (angle between scapular and coracoid glenoid) is correlated with the previous one, with identical codings, so is rejected here and the twelve coded taxa are recoded unknown.

49. Alvarezsaurids (Patagonykus- Novas, 1997; Shuvuuia- pers. obs.) and oviraptorids (Oviraptor, IGM 100/42, "Ingenia"- both unpublished photos) and have tall non-strut-like coracoids. Troodontids (Sinornithoides- Currie and Dong, 2001) and dromaeosaurids (Saurornitholestes- Rauhut, 2003; Deinonychus- Ostrom, 1978) have tall coracoid that are distally expanded. Patagopteryx has a tall coracoid, but it has an unknown amount of distal expansion (Chiappe, 2002).

50. Ornithomimids (Pelecanimimus- unpublished photos; Harpymimus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005), dromaeosaurids (Saurornitholestes- Rauhut, 2003; some Velociraptor- unpublished photo; Deinonychus- Ostrom, 1969), Unenlagia (Novas and Puerta, 1997) and Archaeopteryx (Mayr et al., 2007) have dorsally bowed scapulae. Compsognathus (dorsally bowed in the French specimen- Peyer, 2003), oviraptorids (bowed in IGM 100/42, Rinchenia and "Ingenia"- all unpublished photos), troodontids (bowed in some Troodon specimens- Jasinoski, 2003) and enantiornithines (bowed in Cathayornis- Paul, 2002; Neuquenornis- Chiappe and Calvo, 1994) are polymorphic. The condition in Iberomesornis is uncertain due to limited exposure of the scapula (Sereno, 2000).

51. Compsognathus (Peyer, 2006) lacks a medially displaced supracoracoid foramen. Oviraptorids' ("Ingenia"- Osmolska et al., 2004) are medially displaced.

52. This character should be ordered. Though the derived state is said to be <90 degrees, <60 degrees matches the codings and is used here. Allosaurus (Chure and Madsen, 1996), tyrannosaurids (Gorgosaurus, Albertosaurus, Daspletosaurus- all Makovicky and Currie, 1998), Compsognathus (Peyer, 2006) and troodontids (Sinornithoides- Nesbitt et al., 2009) have a fused furcula with high interclavicular angle. Ornithurae is polymorphic 0+2, since Hesperornis lacks fused clavicles (Marsh, 1880). Ornithomimids and alvarezsaurids (Shuvuuia- Sereno, 2001) probably lack ossified clavicles, so can be coded inapplicable.

53. This character should be ordered. Oviraptorids are polymorphic due to IGM 100/42 and some "Ingenia" lacking fusion between sternal plates (Barsbold, 1983). Iberomesornis has a sternal keel (Sereno, 2000). The presence of a keel or fusion in Patagopteryx is uncertain (Chiappe, 2002). Allosaurus (Chure, 1996), tyrannosaurids (Gorgosaurus and Tyrannosaurus- both Brochu, 2003), Compsognathus (Peyer, 2006), troodontids (Sinornithoides- Currie and Dong, 2001) and Archaeopteryx (Wellnhofer and Tischlinger, 2004) lack ossified sterna, so should be coded inapplicable.
