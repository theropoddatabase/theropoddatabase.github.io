This phylogeny is the author's opinion based on his interpretation of the literature. It is however, broadly congruent with recent works. As most of these animals lie outside my area on expertise, my ideas about them are dependant more on the literature than my own knowledge. In particular, I'm unable to examine controversial phylogenetic issues myself and must rely on measures such as character/taxon number and recency of publication to reconcile contradictory cladograms. The taxa discussed here are those which have been referred to saurischian groups in the past, but are no longer thought to be members of that clade by the author. In addition, animals considered to be carnivorous dinosaurs before the term Theropoda was invented are included, as are taxa considered by an author to be closer to theropods/birds/sauropods/prosauropods than to any other group even if they didn't technically assign the taxon to any of those groups. They are arranged phylogenetically below in what is considered their correct placement today. Ex-saurischian taxa are indicated in bold. Sometimes a junior synonym of a taxon which was never considered to be saurischian was thought to be a saurischian at one time, which explains why taxa like Taniwhasaurus are included. The cladogram is not intended to be an extensive listing of non-saurischian taxa, which are only included to flesh it out and provide reference for the ex-theropod taxa. A few taxa do not fit in the cladogram and are dealt with immediately below. "Dinosaurus" (and perhaps "Aeposaurus") is a typo not based on any real remains, while Succinodon is based on invertebrate shells, which is also probably the case for Macrodontophion.

Sauropodomorphs which used to be thought of as theropods (including most named before 1950) are dealt with in the main sauropodomorph section.
