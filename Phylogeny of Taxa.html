This phylogeny is the author's opinion based on his interpretation of the literature. It is however, broadly congruent with recent works (e.g. Tykoski, 2005; Carrano and Sampson, 2007; Carrano et al., 2012; Loewen et al., 2013; Brusatte et al., 2014; Foth et al., 2014; Lee et al., 2014; Bell and Chiappe, 2015; Lu et al., 2015). The relationships of modern birds are based on molecular studies.

Based roughly on Mike Keesey's old Dinosauricon site. Beta and Gamma are sister taxa within the clade Alpha Delta is within Gamma, but not a member of Zeta. Epsilon, however, is Gamma incertae sedis. *UPDATED* Eta is only uncertainly placed within its HTML page*. Nomina nuda are non-italicized and placed in quotation marks, while unpublished taxa (as recognized by the ICZN) are designated the same way, but followed by an asterisk. Nomina dubia are not distinguished from valid taxa, as which taxa are indeterminate is a subjective question. Taxa in parentheses are technically valid according to the ICZN but not used by most theropod workers.

* Prior to 2021, this site used the question mark to indicate a taxon was tentatively placed in that clade, but this was misleading as only a very basic backbone can be considered a consensus from all well designed analyses. Most taxa instead have a range of possible positions such that a question mark by Epichirostenotes that indicates it may not be a caenagnathine and might actually be an elmisaurine or a more basal caenagnathid is not really comparable to a question mark by Kol that indicates not only might it be outside Avimimidae, it also might be outside Elmisaurinae, Caenagnathidae, Caenagnathoidea, Oviraptorosauria, Pennaraptora and all intermediate clades and actually be nested in Alvarezsauridae. Note this is not yet implemented in Sauropodomorpha and would not work for non-saurischians.
