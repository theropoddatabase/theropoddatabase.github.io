Sereno, Dutheil, Iarochene, Larsson, Lyon, Magwene, Sidor, Varricchio and Wilson, 1996. Predatory dinosaurs from the Sahara and Late Cretaceous faunal differentiation. Science. 272(5264), 986-991.

This paper described Deltadromaeus and the specimen that became the neotype of Carcharodontosaurus. It was Sereno's first published cladistic analysis of avepods, but neither specimen has yet been described in detail.

Taxon issues-

Outgroup- This is stated to be Eoraptor and Herrerasauridae. The latter is based on Staurikosaurus and Herrerasaurus (Sereno and Novas, 1993). Although Sereno considered Chindesaurus a herrerasaurid as early as 1997, it is ignored here as its position is still controversial and Sereno's use of it in 1996 is unknown.
Ceratosauria- As Sereno (1995; 1997) used a sensu lato concept of Ceratosauria including Coelophysoidea, more taxa are used here than would be in most modern analyses. Dilophosaurus, Liliensternus, coelophysids (Procompsognathus, Segisaurus, "Syntarsus" [both rhodesiensis and kayentakatae based on Sereno, 1997] and Coelophysis- based on Sereno, 1999), Ceratosaurus, Elaphrosaurus and abelisaurids (Abelisaurus, Majungasaurus, Carnotaurus- based on Sereno, 1999). Sereno's topology of ((Dilopho(Lilien((Procomp,Segi)(Syntar,Coelo))))(Cerato,Elaphro,Abelidae)) is used.
Torvosauridae- Sereno et al. (1994) mention Eustreptospondylus, Torvosaurus and Chilantaisaurus as non-spinosaurid spinosauroids, and Sereno (1997) include the first two but not Chilantaisaurus among torvosaurids. Thus only Eustreptospondylus and Torvosaurus tanneri are used here. Note this OTU is paraphyletic to Spinosauridae and/or Afrovenator in many analyses.
Spinosauridae- Sereno et al. (1994) specifies Baryonyx and Spinosaurus are used.
Sinraptoridae- Sereno et al. (1994) and Sereno (1997) both state Sinraptor and Yangchuanosaurus are used.
Carcharodontosaurus- Only C. saharicus is used, as C. iguidensis was not discovered yet.
Other coelurosaurs- Based on Sereno (1997), this includes ornithomimosaurs, therizinosauroids, tyrannosauroids, oviraptorosaurs and paravians (alvarezsaurids were still considered controversial as of Sereno, 1997). Ornithomimosaurs presumably include Pelecanimimus, Harpymimus, Garudimimus and ornithomimids. Therizinosauroids here include Alxasaurus and therizinosaurids. Tyrannosauroids only include tyrannosaurids based on Sereno (1999) using only Albertosaurus, tyrannosaurines and with Nanotyrannus (actually a probable juvenile Tyrannosaurus) as the most basal taxon. Oviraptorosaurs are caenagnathoids (Chirostenotes, Elmisaurus, Oviraptor, IGM 100/42, Rinchenia, Conchoraptor, "Ingenia") as Protarchaeopteryx and Caudipteryx had yet to be discovered, and Microvenator and Avimimus had yet to be well resolved. Paraves includes Troodontidae (IGM 100/44, Sinornithoides and troodontines), Dromaeosauridae (Saurornitholestes, Velociraptor, Adasaurus, Deinonychus, Dromaeosaurus, Utahraptor), and Avialae (Archaeopteryx, Confuciusornis, various ornithothoracines) based on publication dates and phylogenetic consensus. Sereno's topology of ((Ornithom,Therizino)(Tyranno(Oviraptoro(Avialae(Troodo,Dromaeo))))) is used. Note this OTU is artificial in many analyses where tyrannosaurids and/or ornithomimosaurs are less closely related to maniraptoriforms than Ornitholestes.

Coding issues-

1. Ceratosaurs (Ceratosaurus- Madsen and Welles, 2000; Abelisaurus- Bonaparte and Novas, 1995; Majungasaurus- Sampson and Witmer, 2007; Dilophosaurus- Tykoski, 2005; "Syntarsus" kayentakatae- Tykoski, 1998), Afrovenator (Benson, 2009) and spinosaurids (Spinosaurus- Dal Sasso, 2005) lack a maxillary fenestra. The same is true for Giganotosaurus (Novas et al., 2013; Coria and Currie, 2006) and Carcharodontosaurus (Brusatte and Sereno, 2007), as their single maxillary opening is like that of Mapusaurus which leads into the promaxillary recess.

2. Monolophosaurus (Brusatte et al., 2010) lacks a pneumatic lacrimal. Spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997) have a pneumatic lacrimal. Ceratosaurs are polymorphic, as ceratosauroids (Ceratosaurus- Madsen and Welles, 2000; Majungasaurus- Sampson and Witmer, 2007) have pneumatic lacrimals. Derived coelurosaurs are also polymorphic in Sereno's topology, as his arctometatarsalians (Erlikosaurus- Witmer, 1997; Harpymimus- Kobayashi, 2004; Garudimimus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005) lack pneumatic lacrimals.

3. Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007- stated to be the same as Dilophosaurus), sinraptorids (Sinraptor- Currie and Zhao, 1994) and derived coelurosaurs (Garudimimus, Struthiomimus and Dromiceiomimus- all coding in Kobayashi, 2004; Erlikosaurus- coding in Sereno, 1999; Conchoraptor- Elzanowski, 1999; Troodon- Currie, 1985; Dromaeosaurus and Deinonychus- both Currie, 1995) lack a ventral lacrimal groove. Monolophosaurus (Brusatte et al., 2010- based on the overlapping lacrimal) has a ventral groove.

4. Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007), Afrovenator (Brusatte et al., 2010), torvosaurids (Torvosaurus- coding in Rauhut, 2003), Allosaurus (Chure, 2000), Ornitholestes (pers. obs. AMNH 619) and derived coelurosaurs (Pelecanimimus- unpublished photo; Garudimimus- Kobayashi, 2004; Erlikosaurus- Clark et al., 1994; Oviraptor and Rinchenia- unpublished photos; Sinornithoides and Saurornithoides- coding in Turner, 2008; Archaeopteryx- coding in Norell et al., 2001) lack a pneumatic jugal.

5. Torvosaurids (Eustreptospondylus- Sadlier et al., 2008), Acrocanthosaurus (Eddy and Clarke, 2011) and derived coelurosaurs (Harpymimus and Garudimimus- coding in Kobayashi, 2004; Erlikosaurus- coding in Sereno, 1999; Dromaeosaurus- Currie, 1995) lack a peg and socket prefrontal-frontal articulation. Allosaurus (Gilmore, 1920; Chure, 1920) has such an articulation. Giganotosaurus and Carcharodontosaurus lack separate prefrontals, so are inapplicable.

6. Spinosaurids (Spinosaurus- Dal Sasso, 2005) and derived coelurosaurs (Erlikosaurus- Clark et al., 1994; Pelecanimimus- Perez-Moreno et al., 1994; Gorgosaurus- Currie, 2003; Archaeopteryx- Wellnhofer, 1974; Velociraptor- Barsbold and Osmolska, 1999) have an antorbital maxillary tooth row. The condition in Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007) is unknown.

7. Torvosaurids (Eustreptospondylus- Sadleir et al., 2008), sinraptorids (Yangchuanosaurus- Dong et al., 1983) and Giganotosaurus (Coria and Currie, 2006) lack a dorsally expanded axial neural spine. Spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997) have an axial spine table.

8. The single complete chevron of Baryonyx lacks anterodorsal processes (Charig and Milner, 1997), so spinosaurids are provisionally coded 0 here, though many other taxa have variation in their tails. Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007) lacks the processes. Ceratosaurs (Ceratosaurus- Madsen and Welles, 2000; Majungasaurus- O'Connor, 2007; Dilophosaurus- Welles, 1984; Segisaurus- Camp, 1936), sinraptorids (Sinraptor? hepingensis- Gao, 1992), Giganotosaurus (coding in Carrano et al., 2012) and Acrocanthosaurus (Stovall and Langston, 1950; Currie and Carpenter, 2000) have some chevrons with anterodorsal processes.

10. Acrocanthosaurus (Currie and Carpenter, 2000) and derived coelurosaurs (therizinosauroids, ornithomimosaurs, oviraptorids, troodontids, Archaeopteryx and Velociraptor, which ensure a basal state of 0 in Sereno's coelurosaur topology despite the narrow metacarpal III in tyrannosaurids, caenagnathids and Deinonychus) have metacarpal III >50% of metacarpal II's width. Torvosaurids (Torvosaurus- Galton and Jensen, 1979) have metacarpal III 50% or less as broad as metacarpal II. Afrovenator (Sereno et al., 1994) and Ornitholestes (Senter, 2006) do not preserve metacarpal III, so cannot be coded.

11. The outgroup (Eoraptor- Sereno et al., 2013; Staurikosaurus- Bittencourt and Kellner, 2009; Herrerasaurus- Novas, 1993), ceratosaurs (Elaphrosaurus- Janensch, 1925; Ceratosaurus- Gilmore, 1920; Dilophosaurus- Tykoski, 2005; Liliensternus- Huene, 1934), Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007- based on pubic articular surface for ilium) and derived coelurosaurs (ornithomimosaurs, tyrannosaurids, Chirostenotes, Rinchenia, IGM 100/42, paravians) have a pubic peduncle articular surface larger than the ischial peduncle articular surface.

12. Spinosaurids (Spinosaurus- Ibrahim et al., 2014) and Monolophosaurus (Zhao et al., 2010) lack an obturator notch. Carcharodontosaurus (Stromer, 1931) has an obturator notch. Torvosaurids (obturator notch in Eustreptospondylus- Sadlier et al., 2008) and sinraptorids (no notch in Yangchuanosaurus- Dong et al., 1983) are polymorphic.

13. Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007) lacks an aliform anterior trochanter. Ceratosaurs (Elaphrosaurus- Rauhut, 2003; Ceratosaurus- Gilmore, 1920; Carnotaurus- Bonaparte et al., 1990; gracile individuals in coelophysoids- Tykoski, 2005) and torvosaurids (Eustreptospondylus- Sadlier et al., 2008) have an aliform anterior trochanter. Ornitholestes (Carpenter et al., 2005) does not preserve the proximal femur.

14. Spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997; Spinosaurus- Stromer, 1934) and Giganotosaurus (coding in Carrano et al., 2012) have a tibia that backs the calcaneum. Ceratosaurs (Elaphrosaurus- coding in Rauhut, 2003; Ceratosaurus- Gilmore, 1920; Majungasaurus- Carrano, 2007) are polymorphic for having a tibia that backs the calcaneum.

15. The outgroup (Eoraptor- Sereno et al., 2013; Herrerasaurus- Novas, 1993), ceratosaurs (Dilophosaurus- Welles, 1984; Liliensternus- Huene, 1934; Ceratosaurus- Gilmore, 1920; Majungasaurus- Carrano, 2007), spinosaurids (Spinosaurus- Ibrahim et al., 2014) and Giganotosaurus (Coria and Salgado, 1995) have a distal fibula less than twice the midshaft anteroposterior width. Sinraptorids are polymorphic (wide distal fibula in Yangchuanosaurus- Dong et al., 1983). The distal fibula of Ornitholestes is unpreserved (AMNH 619).

16. Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007) lacks a plate-like astragalar ascending process. Spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997; Spinosaurus- Stromer, 1934) have a plate-like astragalar ascending process. Ceratosaurs (Ceratosaurus- Madsen and Welles, 2000; Majungasaurus- Carrano, 2007) are polymorphic for having a plate-like astragalar ascending process. Coelurosaurs are uniquely coded an undefined state 2, so are recoded state 1.

17. Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007) has a reduced fibular facet on the astragalus. Ceratosaurs (Ceratosaurus- coding in Carrano et al., 2012; Majungasaurus- Carrano, 2007) are polymorphic for fibular fossa size on their astragalus.

18. Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007) has distally oriented tibiotarsal condyles.

19. Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007) lacks a strong anterior astragalar groove. Acrocanthosaurus (Currie and Carpenter, 2000) has a deep groove. Ceratosaurs (Elaphrosaurus- Rauhut, 2003; Ceratosaurus- Madsen and Welles, 2000) are polymorphic for a strong transverse groove across the astragalar condyles.

20. While determining how much constriction is necessary for an "hourglass-shaped" proximal metatarsal III is subjective, we can use Deltadromeus' condition as a necessary minimum. Afrovenator (Sereno et al., 1994) has only a weakly constricted metatarsal. Derived coelurosaurs (Therizinosaurus- Perle, 1982; Garudimimus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005; Archaeornithomimus- Smith and Galton, 1990; tyrannosaurids) lack an hourglass shape, though they are coded as an undefined state 2, so are recoded 0. Ceratosaurs (state 1 in Elaphrosaurus- Janensch, 1925; Ceratosaurus- Gilmore, 1920) and torvosaurids (state 0 in Eustreptospondylus- Sadleir et al., 2008) are polymorphic. Giganotosaurus does not preserve the main metatarsals so cannot be coded.

21. Torvosaurids (Eustreptospondylus and Torvosaurus- both in Benson and Xu, 2008) and Ornitholestes (Holtz, 1994) lack a trapezoidal metatarsal III shaft. Derived coelurosaurs (Harpymimus and Garudimimus- coding in Kobayashi, 2004; Tyrannosaurus- Holtz, 1994; Elmisaurus- Osmolska, 1981; Sinornithoides- White, 2009) have a trapezoidal shaft.

22. Monolophosaurus (Brusatte et al., 2010) lacks a promaxillary fenestra. Ceratosaurs (Dilophosaurus- Witmer, 1997; Ceratosaurus- Madsen and Welles, 2000; Majungasaurus- Sampson and Witmer, 2007), Afrovenator (Sereno et al., 1994) and spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997; Spinosaurus- Dal Sasso et al., 2005) have a promaxillary fenestra. The outgroup (0 in Eoraptor- Sereno et al., 2013; 1 in Herrerasaurus- Sereno, 2007) and torvosaurids (0 in Torvosaurus- Hendrickx and Mateus, 2014) are polymorphic for the presence of a promaxillary fenestra.

23. Giganotosaurus (coding in Carrano et al., 2012) has a pneumatic ectopterygoid.

24. Spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997; Spinosaurus- Stromer, 1915) and derived coelurosaurs (ornithomimosaurs, therizinosauroids and Archaeopteryx having state 0 means it is primitive in Sereno's topology despite tyrannosaurids and dromaeosaurids having state 1) lack a strongly forked posterior splenial margin. Acrocanthosaurus (Eddy and Clarke, 2011; Harris, 1998) has the forked margin. Sinraptorids are polymorphic (state 0 in Yangchuanosaurus- Dong et al., 1983).

25. Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007) and sinraptorids (Sinraptor dongi- Currie and Zhao, 1994; Yangchuanosaurus- Dong et al., 1983; hepingensis- Gao, 1992) have a mostly dorsally facing m. depressor mandibulae surface on their articular. Giganotosaurus (coding in Carrano et al., 2012) has a mostly posteriorly facing surface. Derived coelurosaurs should be coded as polymorphic, as they have an ambiguous basal state in Sereno's topology (state 0 in ornithomimosaurs, oviraptorosaurs and Archaeopteryx, state 1 in Erlikosaurus, tyrannosaurids and deinonychosaurs).

26. This character is difficult to code, as it only affects chevrons in part of the tail. As Allosaurus first develops an L-shape between the 21st and 25th chevron (Madsen, 1976), taxa with only more proximal chevrons known (e.g. Eoraptor, Segisaurus) cannot be coded. The condition in ceratosaurs is uncertain, as Ceratosaurus seems to lack them (Gilmore, 1920), Majungasaurus has them (O'Connor, 2007), and coelophysiods are universally coded as lacking the condition despite Coelophysis and Dilophosaurus being described as having angled and hatchet-shaped ventral tips respectively (Rinehart et al., 2009; Welles, 1984). Ceratosauria is provisionally coded polymorphic here. Sinraptorids (hepingensis- Gao, 1992) lack L-shaped distal chevrons. Acrocanthosaurus (Currie and Carpenter, 2000) has L-shaped chevrons. Torvosaurids have an unknown state as Eustreptospondylus doesn't preserve chevrons (Sadleir et al., 2008) and Torvosaurus only preserves them up to the seventeenth caudal (Britt, 1991).

27. Clavicles are unpreserved in the outgroup (Eoraptor- Sereno et al., 2013; Herrerasaurus- Sereno, 1993; Staurikosaurus- Bittencourt and Kellner, 2009). Ceratosaurs (Coelophysis, Segisaurus, rhodesiensis, kayentakatae- all Nesbitt et al., 2009) have furculae.

28. Deltadromeus' coracoid has a broad squared end (Sereno et al., 1996). The outgroup is polymorphic (state 0 in Eoraptor- Sereno et al., 2013; state 1 in Herrerasaurus- Brinkman and Sues, 1987). Included torvosaurids lack a coracoid (though if Sereno included Edmarka, the coding would be correct).

29. The outgroup has a cuppedicus fossa (Eoraptor- Sereno et al., 2013; Staurikosaurus- Bittencourt and Kellner, 2009).

30. Spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997), Monolophosaurus (Zhao and Currie, 1994), sinraptorids (Sinraptor dongi- Currie and Zhao, 1994; Yangchuanosaurus- Dong et al., 1983; hepingensis- coding in Carrano et al., 2012) and derived coelurosaurs (Harpymimus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005a; Garudimimus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005b; Enigmosaurus and Segnosaurus- both Zanno, 2010; Gorgosaurus- Lambe, 1917; Tyrannosaurus- Osborn, 1916) lack a pubic peduncle articular surface over twice as long as wide. Acrocanthosaurus (D'Emic et al., 2012) has surface over twice as long as wide. Allosaurus is polymorphic (Benson, 2009).

31. Spinosaurids (Spinosaurus- Ibrahim et al., 2014), Allosaurus (Gilmore, 1920) and derived coelurosaurs (Garudimimus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005; Erlikosaurus- Perle, 1981; Gorgosaurus- Lambe, 1917; Chirostenotes- Sternberg, 1932) have pedal digit I longer than phalanx III-1. Acrocanthosaurus (Currie and Carpenter, 2000) has a short pedal digit I. Ceratosaurs are polymorphic (state 1 in Segisaurus- Camp, 1936; kayentakatae- Tykoski, 1998; rhodesiensis- Raath, 1969; some Coelophysis- Colbert, 1989; notably no ceratosauroids known then could be coded, though we now know Aucasaurus has state 1 as well).

32. Allosaurus (Chure, 2000), Ornitholestes (pers. obs. AMNH 619) and derived coelurosaurs (Pelecanimimus- unpublished photo; Erlikosaurus- Clark et al., 1994; IGM 100/42- unpublished photo; Saurornitholestes- Currie and Varricchio, 2004; Archaeopteryx- Paul, 2002) have an anterior maxillary ramus length at least equal to its height. The outgroup is polymorphic (state 1 in Eoraptor- Sereno et al., 2013).

33. Monolophosaurus (Brusatte et al., 2010) lacks a dorsoventrally narrow anterior lacrimal process. The outgroup (state 1 in Eoraptor) and Allosaurus (state 1 in Britt, 1991) are polymorphic.

34. Spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997) and Acrocanthosaurus (Eddy, 2009) have a dorsally placed lacrimal duct. The outgroup (Eoraptor- Sereno et al., 2013) has a ventrally placed lacrimal duct.

35. Monolophosaurus cannot be coded for postorbital ventral process cross section shape (Brusatte et al., 2010).

36. Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007), spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997) and Acrocanthosaurus (Eddy and Clarke, 2011) have a paraquadrate foramen. Ceratosaurs are polymorphic (no paraquadrate foramen in neoceratosaurs- Ceratosaurus- Madsen and Welles, 2000; Majungasaurus- Sampson and Witmer, 2007; Carnotaurus- Bonaparte et al., 1990).

37. Ceratosaurs are polymorphic (short radius in Majungasaurus- Burch and Carrano, 2008; Carnotaurus- Bonaparte et al., 1990).

38. Acrocanthosaurus (Senter and Robins, 2005) has a short manual ungual I. Allosaurus (Gilmore, 1920), Ornitholestes (AMNH 619) and derived coelurosaurs (Harpymimus- Kobayashi and Barsbold, 2005; Archaeornithomimus- AMNH 6576; Alxasaurus- Russell and Dong, 1994; Segnosaurus- Perle, 1981; Therizinosaurus- Barsbold, 1976; Albertosaurus- Parks, 1928) have an elongate manual ungual I.

39. Spinosaurids (Spinosaurus- Dal Sasso et al., 2005) and Acrocanthosaurus (Eddy and Clarke, 2011) lack antorbital fossa exposure on the nasals. Giganotosaurus (Brusatte et al., 2008) has this exposure. Ceratosaurs are polymorphic (antorbital fossa extends on to nasal in Majungasaurus- Sampson and Witmer, 2007; Dilophosaurus- Smith et al., 2007).

40. The outgroup (Herrerasaurus- Sereno and Novas, 1993), ceratosaurs (Dilophosaurus- Rauhut et al., 2010; kayentakatae- Tykoski, 1998; Ceratosaurus- Madsen and Welles, 2000; Majungasaurus- Carrano and Sampson, 2007; Abelisaurus- Paulina Carabajal, 2011; Carnotaurus- Paulina Carabajal, 2011), torvosaurids (Eustreptospondylus- Sadlier et al., 2008), spinosaurids (Baryonyx- coding in Carrano et al., 2012), Monolophosaurus (Brusatte et al., 2010; as lower pneumatic opening) and derived coelurosaurs (Dromiceiomimus- Tahara and Larrson, 2011; Erlikosaurus- Perle, 1981; Tyrannosaurus- Brochu, 2003; Chirostenotes- Sues, 1997) have excavated internal carotid artery entrances.

41. Contra Sereno et al.'s coding, the basipterygoid processes of e.g. Sinraptor (Paulina Carabajal and Currie, 2012) and Allosaurus (Chure and Madsen, 1996) are not smaller than e.g. rhodesiensis and Ceratosaurus. Spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997) have large basipterygoid processes. Deltadromeus does not preserve cranial material (Sereno et al., 1996) and Afrovenator does not preserve braincase material, so both are coded unknown. If preserved, the basipterygoid processes of Cryolophosaurus are unexposed (Smith et al., 2007). Similarly, the processes of Monolophosaurus were only illustrated in posterior view by Zhao and Currie (1994), making their proportions uncertain as the area is no longer accessable.

42. Spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Hendrickx et al., 2014) have a narrow quadrate-quadratojugal articular surface. Afrovenator (Hendrickx et al., 2014) and Giganotosaurus (Hendrickx et al., 2014) have a broad quadrate-quadratojugal articular surface. Ceratosaurs (broad in Dilophosaurus and Majungasaurus- both Hendrickx et al., 2014), torvosaurids (broad in Eustreptospondylus- Hendrickx et al., 2014) and derived coelurosaurs (broad in Struthiomimus, Alioramus and Dromaeosaurus- all Hendrickx et al., 2014) are polymorphic. The quadrate-quadratojugal articular surface is unexposed in the outgroup (Eoraptor and Herrerasaurus- Hendrickx et al., 2014).

43. Ceratosaurs (rhodesiensis- Bristowe and Raath, 2004; Coelophysis- Witmer, 1997; Majungasaurus- Sampson and Witmer, 2007; Carnotaurus- Paulina Carabajal, 2011), Monolophosaurus (Zhao and Currie, 1994) and Acrocanthosaurus (Eddy and Clarke, 2011) have a dorsoventrally expanded jugal process of the palatine. The posterolateral palatine is unexposed in the outgroup (Eoraptor- Sereno et al., 2013; Herrerasaurus- Sereno and Novas, 1993).

44. Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007), spinosaurids (Baryonx- Charig and Milner, 1997), sinraptorids (Sinraptor dongi- Currie and Zhao, 1994; hepingensis- Gao, 1992) and Giganotosaurus (Coria and Currie, 2002) have basioccipital participation in the basal tubera. The basioccipital-basisphenoid suture is unclear in Monolophosaurus (Brusatte et al., 2010).

45. Acrocanthosaurus (Eddy and Clarke, 2011) does not have a pendant medial articular process.

46. This character is defined as "quadrate short, head near mid-orbit", and coded as derived in only Allosaurus and Monolophosaurus. Only height is coded for here, as placement relative to the orbit also depends on suborbital jugal depth. Given this Monolophosaurus does have a short jugal compared to most taxa (62% of jugal+orbit height), but Allosaurus shows a wide range of 68-85%. If the character is redefined as 70% or less, Allosaurus is then polymorphic. Spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Sereno et al., 1998) have tall quadrates.

47. This character is defined as "surangular twice maximum depth of angular (which equals reduced external mandibular fenestra)", though of the taxa coded with state 1, only Acrocanthosaurus actually has it. A measure which matches Sereno et al.'s codings is whether surangular depth is over half of mandibular depth at the level of the external mandibular fenestra. Ornitholestes (AMNH online) has a deep surangular. Derived coelurosaurs are polymorphic (shallow surangulars in ornithomimosaurs, oviraptorosaurs, IGM 100/44, Saurornithoides, Velociraptor and Deinonychus) The condition in spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Sereno et al., 1998) is very close to 50% but not certain due to the incomplete mandible, so is coded unknown.

49. Monolophosaurus (Brusatte et al., 2010) has a postorbital orbital process. Ceratosaurs (postorbital orbital process in abelisaurids and Dilophosaurus- Tykoski, 2005) and sinraptorids (postorbital orbital process in Sinraptor dongi- Currie and Zhao, 1994; hepingensis- Gao, 1992) are polymorphic.

50. Torvosaurids (Eustreptospondylus- Sadlier et al., 2008; Torvosaurus- Britt, 1991) and Acrocanthosaurus (Eddy and Clarke, 2011) lack a dorsoventrally expanded and squared dentary, which is defined here as having an almost straight anterior border (~20 degrees or less of angling) which is at close to a right angle to the dorsal edge (~80 degrees or more). Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007) and Afrovenator (Sereno et al., 1994) don't preserve dentaries, so cannot be coded.

51. Spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997; Spinosaurus- Ibrahim et al., 2014) have short pubic boots. Sinraptorids are polymorphic (large pubic boot in Yangchuanosaurus- Dong et al., 1983). The condition in Monolophosaurus (Zhao et al., 2010) and Ornitholestes (AMNH 619) is uncertain due to most of the pubic foot being broken.

52. Sinraptorids (Sinraptor dongi- Paulina Carabajal and Currie, 2012; hepingensis- Gao, 1992) have ventrally extensive basisphenoids. Ceratosaurs are polymorphic (ventrally extensive basisphenoids in Dilophosaurus- Welles, 1984; rhodesiensis- Raath, 1977), as are derived coelurosaurs given Sereno's topology (ventrally extensive in ornithomimosaurs, tyrannosaurids, Chirostenotes, Archaeopteryx and Zanabazar). Torvosaurids cannot be coded, as Eustreptospondylus doesn't preserve most of the ventral basisphenoid (Sadlier et al., 2008). That area of the basisphenoid is hidden in Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007).

53. Torvosaurids (Eustreptospondylus- Sadlier et al., 2008; Torvosaurus- Britt, 1991) and spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997) have mid cervical centra with posterior articular surfaces at least 20% broader than tall. As Afrovenator is also coded as lacking this state by Sereno, it is provisionally recoded unknown here. Allosaurus (Madsen, 1976) is polymorphic. The situation in Carcharodontosaurus involves the referral of Sigilmassasaurus vertebrae to the taxon, which has been disproven by Ibrahim et al. (2014). As the only definitely associated cervicals lack described posterior central dimensions and are destroyed (Stromer, 1931), the genus is recoded unknown.

54. Acrocanthosaurus (Harris, 1998) has mid cervical centra with very dorsally displaced anterior surfaces. The outgroup (anterior central face of mid cervicals not displaced dorsally much in Staurikosaurus- Bittencourt and Kellner, 2009; Herrerasaurus- Sereno and Novas, 1993), Monolophosaurus (cervical 7 with low displacement- Zhao and Currie, 1994), sinraptorids (not displaced much in Yangchuanosaurus- Dong et al., 1983) and Allosaurus (cervical 6 with low displacement- Gilmore, 1920) are polymorphic. As with the previous character, no mid cervicals are known for Carcharodontosaurus, with Sereno et al. relying on Sigilmassasaurus (= Spinosaurus anterior dorsals).

55. Cryolophosaurus (coding in Smith et al., 2007), spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997; Spinosaurus- Stromer, 1915), Acrocanthosaurus (Harris, 1998) and Giganotosaurus (Coria and Currie, 2002) lack caudal pleurocoels.

56. Monolophosaurus (Brusatte et al., 2010), Afrovenator (Sereno et al., 1994), torvosaurids (Eustreptospondylus- Sadlier et al., 2008), sinraptorids (Yangchuanosaurus- Dong et al., 1983; Sinraptor dongi- Currie and Zhao, 1994; hepingensis- Gao, 1992) and Allosaurus (Gilmore, 1920) have an extensive antorbital fossa wall. The outgroup (antorbital fossa wall >39% of fossa width in Eoraptor- Sereno et al., 2013) and ceratosaurs (antorbital fossa wall >39% of fossa width in kayentakatae- Tykoski, 1998; Coelophysis- Witmer, 1997; Ceratosaurus- Madsen and Welles, 2000) are polymorphic. The condition in Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007) and spinosaurids is uncertain (no fenestra or fossa wall preserved in Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997; uncertain fenestra length in Spinosaurus- Dal Sasso et al., 2005).

57. Derived coelurosaurs (Garudimimus- Barsbold, 1981; Gorgosaurus and Daspletosaurus- Carr, 2005; Saurornitholestes- Sues, 1978; Deinonychus- Strom, 1969; Velociraptor- Barsbold and Osmolska et al., 1999) have an elongate ventral ectopterygoid fossa. The outgroup (Eoraptor- Sereno et al., 2013) and ceratosaurs (Liliensternus and Coelophysis bauri- Nesbitt, 2011; Ceratosaurus- Madsen and Welles, 2000; Majungasaurus- Sampson and Witmer, 2007) are inapplicable for ectopterygoid fossa shape, as they lack the pneumatic fossa.

58. Deltadromeus (Sereno et al., 1996), spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Mateus et al., 2011) and Ornitholestes (Osborn, 1916) have more than fifteen caudals with short prezygapophyses. Derived coelurosaurs also end up with this state basally (e.g. Harpymimus, Alxasaurus, Tyrannosaurus, caenagnathoids, Archaeopteryx). Monolophosaurus (Zhao et al., 2010) and Carcharodontosaurus (Stromer, 1931) do not preserve enough caudals to code.

59. Allosaurus (0- Chure, 2000; 1- Madsen, 1976) is polymorphic for posteroventral coracoid process length. Giganotosaurus' coracoid is too incomplete to code (Canale et al., 2014), while Acrocanthosaurus' scapulocoracoid fusion makes it impossible to code. Torvosaurids can't be coded as they don't preserve coracoids.

60. Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007) and spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997) have a post-obturator notch. The outgroup (Eoraptor-Sereno et al., 2013; Herrerasaurus- Novas, 1993), Monolophosaurus (Zhao et al., 2010), torvosaurids (Torvosaurus- Galton and Jensen, 1979) and Giganotosaurus (described as lobular by Coria and Salgado, 1995 and when compared to other giganotosaurines) lack a post-obturator notch, which is what makes the obturator flange triangular in coelurosaurs. Sinraptorids (no notch in Yangchuanosaurus- Dong et al., 1983) and Allosaurus (no notch in DINO 11541) are polymorphic.

61. Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007) lacks a pubic obturator notch. Spinosaurids (Spinosaurus- Ibrahim et al., 2014) and Acrocanthosaurus (D'Emic et al., 2011) have a pubic obturator notch. Torvosaurids (notch in Eustreptospondylus- Sadlier et al., 2008) are polymorphic for having obturator notches in the pubis.

62. Deltadromeus (Sereno et al., 1996), torvosaurids (Eustreptospondylus- Sadlier et al., 2008), spinosaurids (Spinosaurus- Ibrahim et al., 2014) and Acrocanthosaurus (Harris, 1998) have unreduced fourth trochanters. Derived coelurosaurs also resolve with this state basally (ornithomimosaurs, Segnosaurus, tyrannosaurids).

63. Spinosaurids (Baryonyx- Charig and Milner, 1997) lack extensive proximomedial fibular fossae. Ornitholestes (AMNH 619) has an extensive fossa. Derived coelurosaurs resolve as uncertain at their base, as ornithomimosaurs and tyrannosaurids have large fossae, but therizinosauroids and oviraptorosaurs+paravians don't.

General analysis conclusions- This analysis is notable for including several recently described taxa (Cryolophosaurus, Monolophosaurus, Afrovenator, Sinraptor, Giganotosaurus). Its main flaws are the assumption of monophyly for some clades (Ceratosauria sensu lato, Torvosauridae, 'other coelurosaurs') and coding OTUs with expected primitive or derived traits. For instance, in the original matrix, the outgroup is all zeroes and Ceratosauria all zeroes except a single 1, but in the revised matrix, the outgroup has six 1's and six polymorphies, while Ceratosauria has eight 1's and fifteen polymorphies. Similarly, 'other coelurosaurs' should have often been coded with polymorphies or 0's based on Sereno's coelurosaur topology, but he seems to have assumed the basal state for many characters. The absence of any coded polymorphies also hindered the analysis. In all, 26% of codings were incorrect, partly due to poor descriptions of Cryolophosaurus, Eustreptospondylus, Baryonyx, Acrocanthosaurus and several coelurosaurs at that time. Notably, Deltadromeus, Afrovenator and Giganotosaurus have yet to be described in detail, so may affect results further once they are. Once the topology is corrected, the result below is found-

The original matrix strongly rejects a ceratosaurian Deltadromeus, but it's almost equally likely to be that as opposed to a tetanurine when recoded. As originally coded, Cryolophosaurus is probably not a ceratosaur and easily acceptable as a non-orionidan tetanurine, but recoding turns this around and makes those two possibilities equally likely while its carnosaurian identity is unlikely. While non-orionidan sinraptorids being recovered in the recoded matrix is unprecedented, this is actually only very weakly rejected in the original, and moving them to Carnosauria in the recoded matrix is about as parsimonious. Oddly, the two currently favored positions for Monolophosaurus (non-orionidan tetanurine or megalosauroid) are both moderately rejected by the original matrix, while placing it in Coelurosauria was weakly rejected. Once recoded, only the megalosauroid option is weakly rejected with the others equally likely. In the original matrix, Afrovenator is weakly rejected as being closer to avetheropods and moderately rejected as being a carnosaur, but recoding makes it weakly rejected as a carnosaur and about equally likely to be closer to Avetheropoda than to torvosaurids. Ceratosaurian torvosaurids (as in Britt, 1991) are very strongly rejected in the original matrix but only weakly rejected once recoded. Carnosaurian torvosaurids (as in Rauhut, 2003) are only very weakly rejected in both. Spinosaurids are moderately rejected as ceratosaurs in the original matrix, but only weakly rejected as non-orionidan tetanurines or carnosaurs. Once recoded, a ceratosaurian position is now weakly rejected, and the other two are almost as well supported as them being megalosauroid. Forcing Afrovenator to be a megalosaurid is about equally parsimonious as Sereno et al.'s position outside Megalosauria.
