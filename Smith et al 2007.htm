Taxon Issues

Plateosaurus- This is assumed to be Plateosaurus engelhardti, and not include gracilis or ingens (which have both been placed in separate genera).
"Syntarsus" kayentakatae- As Syntarsus is a beetle, I call this taxon "Megapnosaurus" kayentakatae.
Coelophysis rhodesiensis- I keep this genus in a separate genus- Megapnosaurus.
Ceratosaurus- This is assumed to include C. nasicornis, C. magnicornis and C. dentisulcatus at least.
Majungatholus- I call this genus Majungasaurus, following Sampson and Krause (eds., 2007).
Irritator- I include Angaturama as a junior synonym.
Allosaurus- This is assumed to include both A. fragilis and A. "jimmadseni".
Sinraptor- This is assumed to include both S. dongi and S. hepingensis.
Carcharodontosaurus- This is assumed to include only C. saharicus, as C. iguidiensis was not described when the manuscript was submitted.
Velociraptor- This is assumed to only include V. mongoliensis, as V. osmolskae was not published yet.
Confuciusornis- This is assumed to include only C. sanctus and C. dui, as C. fedduciae and C. jianchangensis were not published yet.

2. Marasuchus' maxilla is large enough that the complete skull must be at least 50% of femoral length (Sereno and Arcucci, 1994). Zupaysaurus (Ezcurra and Novas, 2007) and Monolophosaurus (Zhao and Currie, 1994) have a skull over half of femoral length. Noasaurus has the plesiomorphic condition (Bonaparte and Powell, 1980), comparing its maxilla and metatarsal length to Masiakasaurus.

4. Silesaurus (Dzik and Sulej, 2007) and Cryolophosaurus (Smith et al., 2007) have a small laterotemporal fenestra.

5. This character (depth of premaxillary body) should be ordered with the states switched to be in order- <0.5 (0); 0.5-1.25 (1); >1.25 (2), thus states 0 and 1 were switched. Herrerasaurus (Sereno and Novas, 1993) has state 2. Dracovenator (Yates, 2005) has state 1. Plateosaurus (Galton, 1984) has state 0.

6. This character is a composite involving both naris placement and the angle of the anterior premaxillary margin. Also the two states do not cover taxa which have angles of 70-75 degrees, so it is altered so that that range is included in state 0. Eoraptor (Langer and Benton, 2006), Plateosaurus (Galton, 1984), Masiakasaurus (Carrano et al., 2011- low angle at least) and Neovenator (Brusatte et al., 2008) have the derived condition. Monolophosaurus is polymorphic, as it has a high angle but long body in front of the naris (Brusatte et al., 2010).

7. Herrerasaurus (coding in Ezcurra, 2006) and Eoraptor (coding in Rauhut, 2003) lack a posteroventral premaxillary process.

8. This character (number of premaxillary teeth) should be ordered, though the state "premaxillary teeth absent" is difficult to position at either end of the series. As it only affects three taxa, two of which form the sister group to the (not included) Pelecanimimus with state 3 (more than five teeth), it is here set as state 4. Ideally premaxillary tooth presence would be a separate character from premaxillary tooth number, but the solution used here only has the slight negative effect of adding a step for Confuciusornis to evolve from other maniraptorans with four premaxillary teeth. Plateosaurus has 5-7 premaxillary teeth (Galton, 1984), so should be coded as polymorphic.

Elaphrosaurus' (Janensch, 1925) and Tugulusaurus' (Rauhut and Xu, 2005) ascending process are between one and two times the body's height, so can be coded 1.

Zupaysaurus (Ezcurra and Novas, 2007) and Streptospondylus (Allain, 2001) have a fossa at the base of their ascending process.
